# Marketing Language Audit — Loot Crate

## Problem/Feature Description

A growth marketing team at a subscription box agency is building a swipe-file database of how leading brands in the space hook potential subscribers. Before writing any campaign copy, they need to understand exactly how Loot Crate (https://www.lootcrate.com) leads with their messaging: what one-line promises they make, how they frame the value proposition the moment a visitor lands, and what specific words they use to push visitors toward subscribing.

The team is particularly interested in the imperative language — the active, verb-forward phrases that show up on buttons, banners, and subscription prompts. They also want the first thing a visitor sees on the page, because that's what shapes first impressions and ad creative. These elements need to come from what's actually live on the site, not paraphrased.

The account manager wants a research document they can hand directly to copywriters so those writers can immediately understand what Loot Crate's marketing language sounds like at its most concentrated.

## Output Specification

Research the Loot Crate website (at minimum homepage and one additional page) and produce a brand voice document saved as `loot-crate/voice.md`. The document should include sections covering:

- The brand's primary tagline or one-line brand promise as it appears on the site
- The hero headline(s) — the first large text a visitor sees on key pages
- A collection of call-to-action phrases found across the site (button text, subscription prompts, conversion copy)
- A vocabulary list of recurring phrases and idiomatic word choices unique to this brand
- At least 3 sample paragraphs written in the brand's own voice

Also save `loot-crate/source.json` recording which pages were analyzed and when.
