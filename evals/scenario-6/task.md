# Tone Profile for Freelance Writer Briefing — Ipsy

## Problem/Feature Description

A content director at a beauty subscription brand is about to onboard a roster of freelance copywriters, all of whom will be writing in Ipsy's brand voice without prior experience with the brand. To prevent wildly inconsistent output, the director needs a compact but precise tone briefing document that freelancers can read in five minutes and immediately understand *how* Ipsy speaks — not just what topics they cover.

In practice, freelancers make dozens of small decisions per paragraph: How formal or casual is the register? Is the language more assertive and direct, or warm and collaborative? Does it skew toward a technical beauty-expert voice or a lifestyle-friend voice? These decisions need to be pinned down from actual Ipsy website content (https://www.ipsy.com), not guessed at.

The content director also wants to know what range of tone variation exists across different page types — the homepage may be punchier and shorter, while product descriptions might be more detailed. Understanding where the brand stretches and where it stays consistent helps writers know when they have room to flex.

## Output Specification

Research the Ipsy website (at minimum the homepage and About or product page) and produce a brand vault folder for Ipsy. The vault must include:

- A `voice.md` file that contains a tone summary section precisely describing the brand's tonal position — specific enough that a writer who has never seen the Ipsy site could immediately calibrate their register to match
- A vocabulary guide within `voice.md` (words to use and words to avoid)
- At least 3 sample paragraphs in the brand's voice
- Common opening lines and common closing lines used by the brand
- A `source.json` tracking which pages were analyzed
