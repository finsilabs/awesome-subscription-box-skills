# New Account Team Onboarding Brief — MeUndies

## Problem/Feature Description

A subscription apparel brand has just brought on three new account managers who will be handling the MeUndies (https://www.meundies.com) account. Before they send a single email or take a single call, every account manager needs to be able to answer the question "so tell me about the MeUndies brand" in thirty seconds — without sounding like they just read a Wikipedia summary.

The brand director wants each new account manager to have a short, punchy onboarding file that tells the essential brand story in compact form: who this brand is (their archetype — the Lover, the Jester, the Hero, etc.), who they're talking to, what visual world they inhabit (the single dominant color that defines them), and what emotional register they operate in (their dominant tone). These five anchors — name, archetype, audience, signature color, tone — are the mental model that lets someone speak confidently about a brand they've never touched before.

In addition to this briefing document, the account managers will also be using automated tools that pull from the brand vault, so the full vault needs to be written and stored correctly.

## Output Specification

Research the MeUndies website and produce a complete brand vault for MeUndies (all standard vault files). In addition to the standard vault, produce a standalone briefing document saved as `brand-brief.md` (inside the vault folder) that provides:

- The brand name
- The brand archetype (using an established archetype framework) and a one-sentence explanation of why it fits
- A one-line audience description covering who they primarily sell to
- The dominant brand color (identified as both a name and a hex value)
- The dominant tone of voice in one or two descriptive words

This briefing document should be brief — the whole thing should be skimmable in under a minute.
