# Subscription Plan Language Research — HelloFresh

## Problem/Feature Description

A product copywriter at a subscription e-commerce consultancy is building a reference library of how leading meal-kit and subscription box brands talk about their plans. The goal is to understand the language patterns competitors use when presenting price points, cadence, flexibility, and value — not to copy them, but to inform how new client brands should position and name their own tiers.

HelloFresh (https://www.hellofresh.com) is one of the most polished subscription brands in the space and has gone through multiple rounds of offer testing. Their website is a live example of how to turn pricing into a story rather than a spreadsheet. The researcher needs to capture exactly how HelloFresh frames their plans: what they call the tiers (if they have them), and the specific language they use to make the subscription feel appealing rather than risky.

This research will be stored as a reusable brand vault entry for HelloFresh that the team can reference whenever a client asks "how does HelloFresh do their pricing copy?"

## Output Specification

Research HelloFresh's website (at minimum the homepage and their plans/pricing page or product selection flow) and produce a brand vault folder named after the brand. The vault should include:

- A voice guide (`voice.md`) that captures the brand's tone and vocabulary
- A dedicated section within the voice guide (or a standalone `pricing-language.md` file) that documents: how HelloFresh names or labels their subscription tiers or plan options, and the persuasive language they use to present the value of subscribing
- A source metadata file (`source.json`) recording which pages were fetched and when
- Sample copy in the brand's voice that demonstrates how they talk about their plans

Clean up any downloaded files over 50 MB before finishing.
