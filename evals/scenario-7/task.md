# Typography Specification for Brand Collateral — Dollar Shave Club

## Problem/Feature Description

A design studio has just been hired to create a suite of branded print and digital assets for Dollar Shave Club (https://www.dollarshaveclub.com) — packaging inserts, social banners, and email headers. Before a single file is opened in the design tool, the creative director needs a detailed type specification derived from how Dollar Shave Club uses typography on their own website, so the studio's work stays consistent with the brand's established visual language.

The studio has had projects go sideways before when they guessed at typographic details. "Sans-serif, bold" isn't enough — they need to know whether the brand uses geometric sans or humanist sans, whether the brand leans on heavy weights or keeps it clean with regular or medium, and whether they play with tight tracking for headlines or prefer more airy spacing. These details are the difference between collateral that feels on-brand and collateral that feels close-but-wrong.

The creative director also needs photo direction for the studio's photographer, since the studio will be producing lifestyle shots to accompany the assets. They want to know how Dollar Shave Club shoots: lighting, subject, mood, setting.

## Output Specification

Research Dollar Shave Club's website (homepage minimum, plus additional pages for richer observation) and produce a brand vault folder with at minimum:

- A `visual.md` guide covering the brand's typographic profile in detail, the photography style, and iconography or illustration style
- A color palette file (`palette.json`) with the brand's colors
- A `source.json` tracking the pages analyzed

The typography section in `visual.md` should be detailed enough that a designer could set type to match the brand without having access to the actual website.
