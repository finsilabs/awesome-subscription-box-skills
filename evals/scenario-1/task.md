# Deep Brand Research — BarkBox

## Problem/Feature Description

Your agency has just onboarded BarkBox (https://www.barkbox.com) as a new client. Before the creative team writes a single word or picks a color, you need to fully understand this brand so that everything the agency produces feels genuinely on-brand.

The account lead wants a thorough research document that covers three angles: how the brand speaks (voice, tone, vocabulary, sample copy), how it looks (visual style — photography, typography, and what imagery to use or avoid), and who it speaks to (audience demographics, what drives them, and what tips them into buying). This isn't a quick overview — the team needs enough detail to actually write in the brand's voice and design to their visual standards without guessing.

You'll need to visit several pages of their website to get a complete picture: the homepage alone won't be enough. Look for their About page, a product page, and any blog or editorial content to understand the full range of their brand expression.

When you're done, also pull representative verbatim phrases directly from the site — the actual words they use — so copywriters have real examples to reference. Credit each phrase with the page it came from so the team can go back to the source.

## Output Specification

Produce a set of brand documentation files including:
- A voice and tone guide with vocabulary recommendations (words to embrace and words to avoid) and sample copy written in the brand's style
- A visual style guide covering typography choices, photography direction, and icon/illustration style
- An audience profile covering who the primary and secondary audiences are, what motivates them, where they are in life, and what triggers a purchase decision
- A quotes file with verbatim phrases from the website, each credited to its source page

All output files should be in a single folder named after the brand.
