# Social Media Imagery Brief — Birchbox

## Problem/Feature Description

A social media team at a beauty brand agency is producing a batch of Instagram and Pinterest content for a new client whose aesthetic they're trying to match against established players in the subscription beauty space. Their creative director wants a reference document showing exactly what visual world Birchbox (https://www.birchbox.com) inhabits — not just colors and fonts, but the photographic and illustrative language they use to represent their brand.

The team has noticed that subscription beauty brands diverge sharply in how they use imagery, and getting the visual direction right before briefing the photographer saves expensive reshoots.

The creative director also wants to understand how Birchbox handles iconography and illustration throughout the site — whether they use custom illustrated elements, simple line icons, or rely purely on photography — because this affects how they approach social graphics and story templates.

## Output Specification

Research the Birchbox website (homepage and at least one additional page) and produce a brand vault folder for Birchbox. The vault must include:

- A `visual.md` guide covering the brand's imagery approach, photography direction (subjects, settings, lighting, mood), and iconography or illustration usage
- A `palette.json` with the brand's colors in the standard format
- A `source.json` recording which pages were analyzed

The imagery guidance in `visual.md` should be specific enough that a photographer reading it could prepare the right mood board before a shoot.
