# Brand Vocabulary Harvest — Cairn

## Problem/Feature Description

A content strategy team is building AI-powered brand-voice tools for outdoor and adventure brands. To train these tools, they need high-quality examples of each brand's distinctive vocabulary — the specific words, phrases, and expressions that make each brand sound like itself rather than a generic outdoor company.

Cairn (https://www.getcairn.com) is an outdoor gear subscription box that has built a distinctive voice around the outdoor lifestyle community. The team wants to understand what language patterns separate Cairn from generic outdoor copy: Do they use specific hiking or outdoor jargon? Do they have a recurring phrase for their community? Are there action words or metaphors they return to again and again? These are the phrases that make copy feel authentic to the brand rather than interchangeable with any competitor.

Crucially, the team wants this vocabulary harvested from what's actually live on the site across multiple page types — not just the homepage, but also the About page and at least one product or blog page — because brands often have slightly different vocabulary registers across different contexts (the homepage might be punchy and aspirational while product pages might be more detailed and practical).

## Output Specification

Research the Cairn website (minimum: homepage, About page, and one product or blog page) and produce a brand vault folder for Cairn. The vault must include:

- A `voice.md` file with a section specifically dedicated to brand vocabulary: idiomatic words and phrases the brand uses (including any outdoor-community language, recurring metaphors, or signature expressions drawn from the actual site copy), and a list of words or phrases that feel off-brand
- At least 3 sample paragraphs in the brand's voice demonstrating the vocabulary in use
- A `source-quotes.md` file in an `examples/` subfolder containing verbatim short phrases pulled from the site, each credited to the page they came from
- A `source.json` recording all pages analyzed (at least 3 URLs)

The vocabulary section in `voice.md` should draw specifically from what was observed across the different page types visited.
