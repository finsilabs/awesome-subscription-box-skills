# Rapid Brand Orientation — Grove Collaborative

## Problem/Feature Description

A brand strategist at a sustainability-focused marketing agency has a pitch meeting in 90 minutes with a prospective client who competes in the eco-friendly household products space. Grove Collaborative (https://www.grove.co) has come up in conversation as a key competitor to reference in the pitch. The strategist needs a fast, usable snapshot of Grove's brand positioning, voice, and visual identity — enough to speak credibly about them in the room — but there is no time for a deep-dive research session across multiple pages.

The strategist's approach for situations like this is to hit the two highest-signal pages — the homepage and the About page — which together usually capture the brand's core promise and personality without requiring a full site crawl. The examples archive can wait for a full engagement. What matters right now is having the core brand files in the vault so the strategy tools can pull them and the strategist can scan them before walking into the meeting.

## Output Specification

Do a rapid brand extraction for Grove Collaborative. Given the time constraint, analyze only the homepage and the About page (do not spend time on product pages, blog posts, or other sections). Produce the standard set of core vault files — voice, visual, color palette, principles, audience, and metadata — but skip the examples subfolder for now.

Save the vault to the standard location used by your marketing stack. Produce a brief `research-log.txt` documenting which pages you visited and in what order, so the strategist can see exactly what the snapshot is based on.
